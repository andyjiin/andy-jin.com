LANGUAGE: C

    •         The MSXII is the 12th generation solar car which is designed and built entirely from scratch by members of the University of Waterloo design team, Midnight Sun. The solar car was built for the American Solar Challenge (ASC) in 2018 and the World Solar Challenge (WSC) in 2019.


            Over the course of 8 months, I helped design the driver display module for the in-vehicle display system. For the first 4 months, I was a part of the hardware team where I designed and prototyped the PCB for the internal driver display board which included features such as an ambient light sensor, step-down DC/DC switching regulator, CAN-to-UART convertor and RTC with crystal oscillator. Because we were designing the board from scratch, a lot of time and effort was put into initial schematic design and hardware part selection.

            For the final 4 months I transferred to the firmware team where I started on the firmware implementation of the driver display module. This included auto-brightness adjustments based on ambient light readings sampled from the ADC and adjusted via PWM and the relaying of information from the CAN bus to the LCD screen.
